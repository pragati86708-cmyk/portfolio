Our Philosophy
01 — Philosophy

The why
behind the build

"Engineering is not merely the application of science — it is the act of shaping the world according to human values."

The Philosophy of Engineering asks us to pause before the blueprint, to question before the code compiles. It situates the engineer not just as a problem-solver, but as a moral agent embedded in society, culture, and time.

In this course, we examine the assumptions beneath technological progress, the responsibilities that come with the power to build, and the human stories that both motivate and are transformed by engineering.

Our portfolio is a record of that inquiry — questions asked, ideas challenged, and perspectives widened.

I
Ethics
Who bears the cost of our creations? Who benefits?
II
Epistemology
How do engineers know what they know?
III
Ontology
What does it mean for a technology to exist in the world?
IV
Politics
Technology is never neutral. Who decides what gets built?
